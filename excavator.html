Hydraulic Excavator

Early Engineering Prototype (Built at Age 11)

The Origin Story

I constructed this project at the age of 11, marking my very first steps into the world of engineering. Inspired by open-source creators on YouTube, I set out to replicate a complex mechanical system using only basic household materials.

This excavator operates on a pure Hydro-Mechanical System using Pascal's principle. It utilizes a network of 6 syringes filled with fluid: three act as the controls (master cylinders), and three act as the actuators (slave cylinders) to move the boom, arm, and bucket.

Despite lacking proper tools, a workshop, or advanced equipment at the time, the desire to create drove me to complete this functional prototype. It remains a personal milestone, representing the spark that ignited my passion for robotics.

Prototype Specs

  • Builder Age: 11 Years Old
  • System: Hydraulic Fluid Power
  • Actuators: 6x Standard Syringes
  • Degrees of Freedom: 3-Axis
  • Materials: Recycled/Handmade
